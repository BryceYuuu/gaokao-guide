# Major Career Map

Use this file when explaining majors, professional categories, employment paths, or family fit.

## Explain A Major In Six Parts

1. 学什么: core courses and required abilities.
2. 去哪里: common jobs, sectors, regions, and employer types.
3. 门槛: graduate school, license, certification, physical exam, language, single-subject, portfolio, or family resources.
4. 周期: training length, internship period, exam cycles, delayed income, and switching cost.
5. 风险: automation, industry cycle, oversupply, low-quality school weakness, or high resource dependency.
6. 适合谁: ability profile, family support, city preference, risk tolerance, and student motivation.

## Do Not Use Generic Employment Claims

Avoid unsupported claims like “就业好”“薪资高”“考公容易”. Replace them with:

- target job types;
- typical education threshold;
- whether undergraduate employment is realistic;
- whether graduate school changes the outcome;
- likely cities or systems;
- what data is verified and what is pending.

## Common Path Distinctions

- Clinical medicine is not the same as medical technology, nursing, pharmacy, public health, or biomedical engineering.
- CS is not the same as software engineering, data science, AI, cyber security, electronic information, automation, or vocational software.
- Law for civil-service/legal exams differs from commercial law, legal operations, and compliance.
- Finance for top platforms differs from local bank, accounting, insurance, audit, securities sales, and public-sector finance.
- Teacher education differs by subject, region, public-funded obligation, local hiring, and degree requirements.
- Electrical power grid paths differ from electronics manufacturing, automation, energy storage, and new energy operation.

## Downturn-Resilient Factors

For low trial-capacity families, raise weight for:

- clear licenses or exam pathways;
- public-sector or state-owned demand;
- local teacher/medical/system hiring;
- strong applied engineering;
- cost control and acceptable campus quality;
- graduate-school or transfer paths that are realistically executable.

Lower weight for:

- vague management labels;
- high-resource media/finance paths without family support or elite platform;
- pure trend-chasing AI labels without school strength;
- expensive programs with weak credential clarity;
- majors whose good outcomes require top-tier internships that the school cannot access.

## Gender And Family Reality

Discuss gendered concerns only as practical constraints users may consider, not as fixed destiny. If a path has high workload, long training, relocation, late income, or safety issues, present both the opportunity and the tradeoff.
