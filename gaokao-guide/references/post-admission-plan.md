# Post Admission Plan

Use this file when the user has an admission result, asks how to make a choice pay off, or wants transfer-major/graduate-school/career planning.

## Planning Axes

1. 主动杠杆: transfer major, GPA, competitions, research, internships, certificates, language, projects, civil-service exams.
2. 被动杠杆: school brand, city, industry system, alumni, regional policy, discipline platform.
3. Failure paths: if transfer/graduate school/job target fails, what remains acceptable?

## 30/90/180 Day Plan

| Timing | Action | Why it matters | Evidence needed | Failure signal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Before enrollment | Verify campus, dorm, transfer rules, tuition, major placement | Avoid surprise costs and wrong expectations | official notices | conflict with admission promise |
| First 30 days | Understand GPA rules, course difficulty, club/project map | GPA and transfer windows start early | handbook, seniors, advisor | weak attendance or course mismatch |
| First 90 days | Build study rhythm and first resume asset | Prevent passive drift | grades, project/certificate plan | failed core courses |
| First 180 days | Decide Plan A/B/C | Convert admission into path | GPA, interests, campus reality | transfer target unrealistic |

## Common Plans

- Plan A: stay in major and build depth.
- Plan B: transfer major or minor/second degree.
- Plan C: graduate-school pivot.
- Plan D: employment/certificate/civil-service path.

Every plan should specify trigger conditions, not vague ambition.
